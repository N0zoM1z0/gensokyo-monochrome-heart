# Myouren Temple and Cemetery
## 命蓮寺・墓地

## Canon anchor

A Buddhist temple founded by Byakuren and inhabited or visited by several youkai, with a nearby cemetery and the Palanquin Ship legacy.

This file uses the anchor as a foundation and treats all spot layouts and incidents below as project-original.

## Narrative thesis

**Community is not sameness; hospitality requires rules and repair.**

## Exploration grammar

Community schedules, chants, cloud platforms, anchor momentum, and surprise setups create varied traversal.

### Companion affordances

At least three companions in this region must alter traversal in different ways. A companion action should reveal personality or relationship state; it must not function as a generic colored key.

### State readability

The region needs four visible world-state changes:
1. an incident-state alteration;
2. a route-private alteration;
3. a seasonal alteration;
4. a post-finale “imperfect but alive” alteration.

## Danmaku language

Prayer rings, fists in clouds, anchors, unidentified silhouettes, and bell-wave timing.

## Fighting stage

Temple yard with Community Rule condition: collateral damage reduces both fighters' meter.

## Event hooks

- A chant repeats words no one spoke.
- Kogasa finally surprises someone and regrets the result.
- Nue makes a memory unidentifiable rather than false.
- Murasa finds a passenger list from a voyage that never occurred.

## 1-bit art direction

temple beams, bell rope, grave markers, cloud fists, ship planks and anchor chain

### Dither allocation

- 0%: important white space, sky, or quiet emotional focus
- 25%: distant atmosphere
- 50%: traversable but uncertain space
- 75%: danger, rumor, or occluded depth
- 100%: solid silhouette and collision-critical geometry

Never use high-frequency dither directly behind bullets or dialogue text.

## Audio direction

Chant-derived rhythm, nautical pulse, comic umbrella stings, warm communal ending cue.
