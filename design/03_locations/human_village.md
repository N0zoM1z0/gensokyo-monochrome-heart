# Human Village
## 人間の里

## Canon anchor

Gensokyo's principal human settlement, containing shops, schools, the Hieda residence, Suzunaan, and many ordinary lives shaped by youkai rumors.

This file uses the anchor as a foundation and treats all spot layouts and incidents below as project-original.

## Narrative thesis

**Public safety, curiosity, and truthful history rarely align perfectly.**

## Exploration grammar

Crowd schedules and rumor states change dialogue and available alleys. The protagonist's public reputation matters here.

### Companion affordances

At least three companions in this region must alter traversal in different ways. A companion action should reveal personality or relationship state; it must not function as a generic colored key.

### State readability

The region needs four visible world-state changes:
1. an incident-state alteration;
2. a route-private alteration;
3. a seasonal alteration;
4. a post-finale “imperfect but alive” alteration.

## Danmaku language

Rare; used for contained youma-book incidents and rumor silhouettes rather than open combat.

## Fighting stage

Festival square or village edge only; bystanders impose spell-card safety boundaries.

## Event hooks

- A chronicle entry describes a private scene.
- A demon book opens to the protagonist's future route ending.
- A harmless rumor causes real patrols.
- Keine must choose between preserving and correcting a night of history.

## 1-bit art direction

shop signs, lattice doors, paper lanterns, carts, school boards, crowded low silhouettes

### Dither allocation

- 0%: important white space, sky, or quiet emotional focus
- 25%: distant atmosphere
- 50%: traversable but uncertain space
- 75%: danger, rumor, or occluded depth
- 100%: solid silhouette and collision-critical geometry

Never use high-frequency dither directly behind bullets or dialogue text.

## Audio direction

Warm market pulse; quiet paper-and-brush cue for archives; festival variations.
