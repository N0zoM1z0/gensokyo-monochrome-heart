# Hidden Back Doors and Boundary Spaces
## 後戸・境界空間

## Canon anchor

Secret routes associated with Okina's back doors and Yukari's boundaries connect places that ordinary geography cannot.

This file uses the anchor as a foundation and treats all spot layouts and incidents below as project-original.

## Narrative thesis

**Access is power; privacy depends on who controls the door.**

## Exploration grammar

Screen edges connect, objects gain backs, exits depend on season energy, and UI frames become traversable space.

### Companion affordances

At least three companions in this region must alter traversal in different ways. A companion action should reveal personality or relationship state; it must not function as a generic colored key.

### State readability

The region needs four visible world-state changes:
1. an incident-state alteration;
2. a route-private alteration;
3. a seasonal alteration;
4. a post-finale “imperfect but alive” alteration.

## Danmaku language

edge-wrap bullets, doors behind bullets, season quadrants, eye motifs used sparingly.

## Fighting stage

Abstract frame stage; Boundary Leak condition connects selected edges.

## Event hooks

- A door opens onto a scene the player declined.
- Okina treats the protagonist's return as unauthorized access.
- Yukari cannot close a border without erasing one shared memory.
- Kasen disputes whether the safest barrier is the most humane.

## 1-bit art direction

UI frames, door silhouettes, eye-like negative space, rail lines, floating seasonal icons

### Dither allocation

- 0%: important white space, sky, or quiet emotional focus
- 25%: distant atmosphere
- 50%: traversable but uncertain space
- 75%: danger, rumor, or occluded depth
- 100%: solid silhouette and collision-critical geometry

Never use high-frequency dither directly behind bullets or dialogue text.

## Audio direction

Unstable meter, reversed phrase fragments, silence used as a structural beat.
