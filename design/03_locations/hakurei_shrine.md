# Hakurei Shrine
## 博麗神社

## Canon anchor

A shrine at Gensokyo's border and Reimu's home. It functions as a threshold between Gensokyo and the Outside World as well as a social gathering point.

This file uses the anchor as a foundation and treats all spot layouts and incidents below as project-original.

## Narrative thesis

**Home is proved by repeated responsibility, not ownership.**

## Exploration grammar

Balanced, readable geometry; chores reveal supernatural traces. Wind, leaves, paper charms, guest shoes, and small repairs are state indicators.

### Companion affordances

At least three companions in this region must alter traversal in different ways. A companion action should reveal personality or relationship state; it must not function as a generic colored key.

### State readability

The region needs four visible world-state changes:
1. an incident-state alteration;
2. a route-private alteration;
3. a seasonal alteration;
4. a post-finale “imperfect but alive” alteration.

## Danmaku language

Wide negative spaces, amulet arcs, boundary gaps, leaf bullets, and patterns that punish greed rather than hesitation.

## Fighting stage

Flat yard with shrine steps, donation box collision prop, and Boundary Leak condition.

## Event hooks

- An offering contains someone else's memory.
- A cushion remains prepared for a guest no one admits expecting.
- A festival rumor attracts the wrong kind of faith.
- The boundary opens only when nobody watches.

## 1-bit art direction

weathered wood, paper screens, rope, bell, stone, weeds, maple and cherry leaves; large white sky fields contrast dense black eaves

### Dither allocation

- 0%: important white space, sky, or quiet emotional focus
- 25%: distant atmosphere
- 50%: traversable but uncertain space
- 75%: danger, rumor, or occluded depth
- 100%: solid silhouette and collision-critical geometry

Never use high-frequency dither directly behind bullets or dialogue text.

## Audio direction

Sparse shrine-bell pulse; arrangement reference: Reimu-associated themes, with original melody preferred for core narrative.
