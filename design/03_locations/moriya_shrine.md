# Moriya Shrine
## 守矢神社

## Canon anchor

A shrine relocated to Youkai Mountain, associated with Sanae Kochiya and the gods Kanako Yasaka and Suwako Moriya.

This file uses the anchor as a foundation and treats all spot layouts and incidents below as project-original.

## Narrative thesis

**Faith cannot be reduced to audience size, technology, or nostalgia.**

## Exploration grammar

Modern planning tools coexist with old ritual spaces. Visitor flow and power routing create spatial puzzles.

### Companion affordances

At least three companions in this region must alter traversal in different ways. A companion action should reveal personality or relationship state; it must not function as a generic colored key.

### State readability

The region needs four visible world-state changes:
1. an incident-state alteration;
2. a route-private alteration;
3. a seasonal alteration;
4. a post-finale “imperfect but alive” alteration.

## Danmaku language

Snake curves, frog rings, pillar lanes, miracle telegraphs, and faith symbols that change behavior when observed.

## Fighting stage

Festival platform with Divine Audience condition; crowd response changes meter gain.

## Event hooks

- A successful publicity campaign creates belief in the wrong miracle.
- The ropeway brings a visitor from a memory that never happened.
- Sanae finds an Outside World object she no longer recognizes.
- Kanako and Suwako disagree over what must remain inefficient.

## 1-bit art direction

shimenawa, lake horizon, wind machinery, pillars, frog and snake motifs, clean mountain sky

### Dither allocation

- 0%: important white space, sky, or quiet emotional focus
- 25%: distant atmosphere
- 50%: traversable but uncertain space
- 75%: danger, rumor, or occluded depth
- 100%: solid silhouette and collision-critical geometry

Never use high-frequency dither directly behind bullets or dialogue text.

## Audio direction

Bright festival rhythm with wind and metallic pulse; softer harmonium-like night cue.
