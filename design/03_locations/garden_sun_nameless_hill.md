# Garden of the Sun and Nameless Hill
## 太陽の畑・無名の丘

## Canon anchor

Flower-rich regions associated with Yuuka and poisonous lilies of the valley; beauty and danger coexist.

This file uses the anchor as a foundation and treats all spot layouts and incidents below as project-original.

## Narrative thesis

**Attention is a form of respect when it does not become possession.**

## Primary cast

- Yuuka
- Medicine cameo
- Hina visitors
- Alice visitors

## Spots

- Sunflower Rows

- Lily Valley
- Abandoned Doll Site

## Exploration grammar

Plant rhythms, shadow angles, poison-safe paths, and seasonal microchanges.

### Companion affordances

At least three companions in this region must alter traversal in different ways. A companion action should reveal personality or relationship state; it must not function as a generic colored key.

### State readability

The region needs four visible world-state changes:
1. an incident-state alteration;
2. a route-private alteration;
3. a seasonal alteration;
4. a post-finale “imperfect but alive” alteration.

## Signature minigames

- Flower Listening
- Shadow Watering
- Poison Wind Route
- Doll Memorial

## Danmaku language

Petal geometry, sunflower rays, pollen fields, umbrella beam silhouettes.

## Fighting stage

Sunflower lane with Bloom Cycle condition; terrain changes slowly and predictably.

## Event hooks

- Flowers reproduce a private conversation as growth patterns.
- Yuuka destroys a perfect artificial bloom.
- A doll remembers being abandoned by someone who never existed.
- The Archive cannot classify a seed that has not sprouted.

## 1-bit art direction

repeating flower heads differentiated by silhouette, umbrella arc, dense grasses, poison bells

### Dither allocation

- 0%: important white space, sky, or quiet emotional focus
- 25%: distant atmosphere
- 50%: traversable but uncertain space
- 75%: danger, rumor, or occluded depth
- 100%: solid silhouette and collision-critical geometry

Never use high-frequency dither directly behind bullets or dialogue text.

## Audio direction

Patient floral ostinato with sudden powerful bloom chord; night insects for afterbeats.
