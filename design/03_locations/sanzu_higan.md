# Sanzu River and Higan
## 三途の川・彼岸

## Canon anchor

The Sanzu River is crossed by the dead under shinigami guidance; Higan is associated with judgment by Eiki Shiki.

This file uses the anchor as a foundation and treats all spot layouts and incidents below as project-original.

## Narrative thesis

**A finite life cannot be valued by length alone, and judgment requires context.**

## Primary cast

- Komachi
- Eiki
- departing spirits
- Yuyuko and Youmu in route events

## Spots

- Unfinished Shore

## Exploration grammar

Distance changes according to story weight. Inventory items become testimony rather than tools.

### Companion affordances

At least three companions in this region must alter traversal in different ways. A companion action should reveal personality or relationship state; it must not function as a generic colored key.

### State readability

The region needs four visible world-state changes:
1. an incident-state alteration;
2. a route-private alteration;
3. a seasonal alteration;
4. a post-finale “imperfect but alive” alteration.

## Signature minigames

- Ferry Fare

- Testimony Ordering
- Distance Knot

## Danmaku language

Coin and soul lanes, moral black/white patterns that reveal gray through spacing, ferry wake arcs.

## Fighting stage

Court approach only; Judgment condition displays repeated habits as buffs and debuffs.

## Event hooks

- The protagonist's name appears on a crossing list too early.
- Komachi delays a crossing for a reason she refuses to call compassion.
- Eiki finds a perfectly consistent record suspicious.
- A memory asks to be judged as a person.

## 1-bit art direction

wide river bands, coin glints, red-spider-lily silhouettes, court steps, vast white fog

### Dither allocation

- 0%: important white space, sky, or quiet emotional focus
- 25%: distant atmosphere
- 50%: traversable but uncertain space
- 75%: danger, rumor, or occluded depth
- 100%: solid silhouette and collision-critical geometry

Never use high-frequency dither directly behind bullets or dialogue text.

## Audio direction

Measured ferry rhythm, low bell, spare judgment motif with unresolved cadence.
