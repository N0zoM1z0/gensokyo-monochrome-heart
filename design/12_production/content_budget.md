# Content and Asset Budget

## 1. Scope tiers

### Vertical slice
- 1 headline region / 1 main spot;
- 2 primary characters;
- 1 exploration map;
- 1 minigame;
- 1 three-phase danmaku encounter;
- 1 compact duel;
- 1 Keepsake and Journal thread;
- 25–40 minutes;
- EN/JA complete;
- geometric placeholders, then one style proof.

### v0.8 / early access candidate
- 5 headline regions;
- 12 deep-route characters partially/fully available according to release strategy;
- 50 authored spot events;
- 10 danmaku player/boss loadouts;
- 8 fighters;
- 25–35 minigames in production quality;
- 15–25 hours first run.

### v1.0 target
- 19 region bibles, with 5–8 high-depth regions and others at support/postgame depth;
- 71 character profiles integrated;
- 12 deep routes plus Ensemble Accord;
- 100–120 spot events;
- 50 minigame concepts, of which 30–40 need unique implementation and the rest can be variants;
- 89 music cue slots, many sharing adaptive arrangements/stems;
- 30–45 hours route-completion playtime.

## 2. Event budget by class

| Event class | Duration | Unique mechanics | Text range EN | Typical asset load |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| micro spot | 3–6 min | none or existing | 200–600 words | existing map/portraits |
| support event | 8–15 min | one variation | 700–1,800 words | 1–3 props/poses |
| main event | 15–30 min | one major mode | 1,500–3,500 words | map changes, VFX, cue |
| route finale | 25–45 min | major mode + afterbeat | 2,500–5,000 words | bespoke poses/cue/replay |
| chapter climax | 30–60 min | multi-mode | 3,500–7,000 words | boss/pattern/transition |

Japanese is not budgeted as a simple word multiplier; allocate independent editorial time.

## 3. Character art budget

### Deep route lead
- Model M base + work/weather/formal variants;
- 8–10 exploration gestures;
- 9–12 portrait expressions plus props;
- danmaku set if playable/boss;
- Model L fighter set if in launch eight;
- Journal stamp and route object.

### Support character
- Model M base;
- 3–5 gestures;
- 5–7 portraits;
- boss or companion set only where required.

### Cameo
- Model S/M minimal;
- 2-frame idle/entrance;
- silhouette or 3-expression portrait pack.

## 4. Environment budget

Headline region:
- 5–8 reusable rooms/spots;
- 1 terrain, 1 architecture, 1 prop atlas;
- calm/night/incident variants;
- 12–20 interactive props;
- 3 weather/incident overlays;
- 1 boss/duel arena;
- 1 quiet afterbeat composition.

Support region:
- 2–4 spots;
- mostly shared systems;
- one signature mechanic and visual noun set.

## 5. Audio budget

Do not commission 89 entirely separate full arrangements automatically.

Recommended:
- 12–18 region/character arrangement families;
- each family supplies place/person/incident stems and 1–3 afterbeat tails;
- 8–12 combat arrangements;
- 6 system/original cues;
- 100–160 SFX source designs expanded through controlled variation;
- separate mastering and loop QA.

## 6. Text budget risk

At 100 events, average 1,500 EN words already implies 150,000 words before UI, Journal, alternate branches, and Japanese. Keep event lengths disciplined. Use environmental action and mechanics to carry characterization rather than writing every beat as dialogue.

## 7. Cut order if schedule slips

1. reduce unique postgame minigames by using variants;
2. move secondary regions to post-launch updates;
3. reduce launch fighter roster, never remove story accept-loss;
4. reduce costume variants;
5. move Dream Theatre to postgame/update;
6. reduce support event count;
7. preserve main campaign, 12 route promises only if already publicly committed, EN/JA quality, accessibility, saves, and legal compliance.
