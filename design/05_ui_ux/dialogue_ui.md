# Dialogue UI

## 1. Layout

At 320 × 180:
- portrait stage occupies 88 px on the speaking side;
- text panel spans 216–304 px depending on scene framing;
- three visible EN lines or four compact JA lines;
- speaker name is a tab attached to the panel, not floating text;
- important sound, pause, or gesture can occupy a dedicated cue line.

The system supports:
- left speaker;
- right speaker;
- two-speaker overlap;
- no portrait / environmental narration;
- silhouette portrait for uncertain identity;
- small companion reaction bubble.

## 2. Text behavior

- Text appears per grapheme cluster, not byte or code point.
- Punctuation-aware pauses are authored or generated by locale rules.
- Player may set instant text.
- Auto mode waits on voice-free reading-time estimates and never advances choices.
- Backlog stores the last 200 accepted lines plus nonverbal cues.
- Important nouns may receive a stamped glossary marker, not colored text.

## 3. Choice design

Choices communicate **tone and action**:

```text
DIRECT   “Ask why the second cup is still warm.”
PLAYFUL  “Claim the cup has been waiting more patiently than she has.”
PATIENT  “Pour tea and leave the empty place open.”
DEFIANT  “Say the shrine cannot keep pretending nothing changed.”
```

Rules:
- no “correct” color;
- no heart icons;
- no hidden timeout unless the scene is explicitly about hesitation;
- a choice can improve one facet and strain another;
- unavailable tones explain the in-world reason (“You promised not to joke about this”).

## 4. Portrait direction

Portraits use three layers:
- base bust silhouette;
- eyes/mouth micro-expression;
- prop or environmental overlay.

Avoid collecting dozens of generic emoji-like faces. Each character gets 6–10 expressions selected from their profile, including at least two neutral working states and one non-romantic positive state.

## 5. Resonance feedback

After a meaningful beat, show an object-based tell for 0.6–1.2 seconds:
- Reimu slides the second cup closer;
- Marisa returns one borrowed item without announcing it;
- Sakuya leaves one minute uncorrected;
- Aya lowers the camera;
- Kaguya allows the clock to advance;
- Youmu lets one petal land.

The Journal records the observation. It does not say “Trust +1.”

## 6. Japanese typography

- use a licensed bitmap or pixel-compatible Japanese font with complete JIS coverage;
- prohibit faux-bold raster scaling;
- enable kinsoku shori for opening/closing punctuation;
- do not split ruby annotations across lines;
- avoid excessive Latin abbreviations in Japanese UI;
- allow speaker-name width to expand to 72 px before reducing font size.

## 7. Comfort-sensitive writing

Content toggles may soften or replace:
- intoxication-centered jokes;
- medical needle imagery;
- aggressive romantic pursuit;
- jump scares;
- body-horror silhouettes.

Replacement lines preserve plot function and route state. A toggle is not allowed to silently skip required information.
