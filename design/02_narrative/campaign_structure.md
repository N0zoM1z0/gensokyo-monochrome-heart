# Main Campaign Structure

## Prologue — Dream Wakes Twice

### Opening
The protagonist lives alone in the Outside World. The old charm, doll, and petal are the only evidence of a vanished season. A monochrome image appears on a dead phone screen: the Hakurei Shrine donation box, with one empty cushion beside it.

### Crossing
The protagonist follows a sound that resembles both a train crossing and shrine bells. The screen folds. They awaken at the shrine.

### First contradiction
Reimu recognizes them before pretending not to. Marisa arrives loudly and ruins the moment. Neither remembers the same version of the protagonist's earlier visit.

### Tutorial
- shrine exploration
- Direct / Playful / Patient / Defiant choice
- Leaf-Graze Sweep
- micro danmaku tutorial against a boundary stain
- first Journal entry

## Chapter 1 — The Shrine Keeps an Empty Place

### Regional thesis
Ordinary habits prove a relationship better than grand declarations.

### Key events
- repair the unused cushion;
- investigate offerings that contain memories instead of objects;
- stop a rumor that the protagonist is a divine messenger;
- Reimu route event: “Tea Before the Incident”;
- Marisa route event: “A Borrowed Object Returned Too Soon”;
- first duel: Reimu vs Marisa, protagonist mediates spell-card conditions.

### Chapter reveal
The memory stains are generated when private habits are described as public facts.

## Chapter 2 — A Clock Loses One Minute

### Regional thesis
Perfection can hide exhaustion and erased mistakes.

### Key events
- a corridor repeats one minute at dusk;
- Sakuya has been correcting the mansion so aggressively that the missing minute has become physical;
- Patchouli discovers books with pages describing conversations before they happen;
- Remilia publicly claims to have foreseen everything;
- Flandre identifies a “breakable” point in the record system.

### Mechanical climax
Time-grid service minigame into Sakuya danmaku.

### Chapter reveal
The archive is fed by revisions: every time someone replaces a messy memory with a cleaner story, the monochrome system grows.

## Chapter 3 — Tomorrow Arrives as a Newspaper

### Regional thesis
Information can protect, exploit, connect, or flatten.

### Key events
- Aya publishes a photograph of an event that has not occurred;
- Hatate retrieves images based on keywords that everyone is now using;
- Nitori builds a rumor duplicator;
- Momiji is ordered to guard a path against a threat no one can describe;
- Sanae turns the incident into a faith campaign, then realizes the campaign is changing the incident.

### Mechanical climax
Photo-graze chase through mountain wind lanes.

### Chapter reveal
The system predicts the future by making people perform the version already published.

## Chapter 4 — The Bamboo Night Refuses to End

### Regional thesis
Eternity without choice is repetition.

### Key events
- Eientei corridors loop through alternative versions of the same evening;
- Reisen hears wavelengths from memories that did not happen;
- Tewi sells shortcuts that lead to emotionally inconvenient truths;
- Eirin studies the protagonist's keepsakes;
- Kaguya proposes preserving one perfect night forever;
- Mokou burns a path through a false moon, destroying both useful and harmful records.

### Mechanical climax
Five Impossible Errands followed by a false-moon danmaku encounter.

### Chapter reveal
The protagonist's return was possible because their remembered year became unusually dense at the boundary. The same density now threatens to fossilize Gensokyo.

## Chapter 5 — Petals Remember How to Fall

### Regional thesis
Letting go is an active form of care.

### Key events
- spirits cannot leave because every farewell is replayed;
- Youmu over-maintains the garden to prevent one petal from landing;
- Yuyuko treats the repetition lightly until a meal never ends and therefore loses all meaning;
- the protagonist must choose which of their own memories to release;
- Komachi and Eiki appear at the edge of the route to question the ethics of borrowed time.

### Mechanical climax
Soul Garden into paired Youmu duel.

### Chapter reveal
Forgetting is not betrayal. A memory needs an ending to remain a memory.

## Chapter 6 — The Border Behind the Screen

### Regional thesis
A world cannot be saved by making it unable to change.

### Structure
Cross-faction event chain:
- public hearing in the Human Village;
- reporter confrontation;
- library and chronicle audit;
- boundary-fold expedition;
- three character route promises are called into question;
- player decides the rules for the Memory Archive.

### Final boss form
The Archive manifests as composite patterns built from the player's most repeated strategies. It uses familiar safe spaces, then removes them.

### Final action
The protagonist cannot destroy it alone. Characters the player understood contribute mechanics:
- Reimu creates an unrecorded gap;
- Marisa overloads the index;
- Sakuya gives back the missing minute;
- Aya lowers the camera;
- Kaguya releases the endless instant;
- Youmu cuts the attachment, not the memory;
- others contribute according to completed routes.

## Epilogue — A World Drawn in One Bit

Color does not suddenly return. Instead, black and white regain texture:
- imperfect lines;
- changing weather;
- handwritten notes;
- unrepeatable timing;
- blank pages intentionally left blank.

The player chooses an ending family.
