# Humor and Meme Register

## Humor layers

### Layer 1 — Situational
Works for every player:
- a task is harder because of an ability;
- etiquette collides with practicality;
- two competent people optimize in incompatible ways.

### Layer 2 — Character callback
Requires earlier game knowledge:
- a returned object;
- an old promise;
- a familiar recovery line.

### Layer 3 — Fandom wink
Optional and restrained:
- donation-box anxiety;
- ze / daze cadence;
- “China” misunderstanding;
- PAD rumor;
- NEET Kaguya;
- Myon;
- ⑨.

Layer 3 must never carry the emotional resolution.

## Meme intensity scale

- **0 — none:** serious or low-canon-confidence scene
- **1 — background:** item label, NPC rumor, one reaction
- **2 — scene spice:** one beat with character pushback
- **3 — premise:** whole event begins from the meme, then complicates it
- **4 — dream theatre only:** broad parody
- **5 — prohibited in main continuity**

Each `skills.md` specifies a maximum.

## Running-gag limits

A running gag may recur:
- once per region chapter;
- after a meaningful variation;
- only if the character has other scene functions.

## Joke ownership

Whenever possible, the target character gets the final word.

Example:
- NPC repeats a “perfect maid” body rumor.
- Sakuya does not become embarrassed for the player's entertainment.
- She calmly produces contradictory measurements from three stopped timelines, making the rumor-mongers the joke.
- Private afterbeat returns to the burden of being constantly observed.

## Translation humor

Do not force Japanese puns into literal English. Use:
- functional equivalent;
- brief glossary note;
- alternate joke written for the English line;
- preserved proper-noun joke when recognizable.

Japanese and English may have different punch-line wording while preserving intent and route state.
