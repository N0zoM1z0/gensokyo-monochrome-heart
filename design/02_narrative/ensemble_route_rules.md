# Ensemble Route and Harem-Comedy Rules

## Purpose

The source material strongly values an “everyone together” fantasy. The game supports that fantasy while preserving character agency and avoiding a conquest scoreboard.

## Two separate systems

### Ensemble comedy
Available throughout:
- festivals;
- scheduling collisions;
- group meals;
- faction competition;
- playful jealousy;
- cooperative incident resolution.

This does not imply romance with everyone.

### Ensemble Accord
Optional postgame relationship structure:
- friendship and romance choices are explicitly remembered;
- no character is silently enrolled;
- practical boundaries are discussed;
- the protagonist accepts labor, time, and emotional responsibilities;
- characters retain independent relationships and priorities.

## Accord prerequisites

- six or more deep routes completed;
- at least three must be friendship endings;
- no severe unresolved Strain;
- “rank the cast” spectacle refused;
- at least one promise postponed rather than falsely accepted;
- three cross-faction repair events;
- protagonist has chosen a home responsibility.

## Accord scene outline

1. Aya's rumor implies an imminent “official choice.”
2. Competing public performances begin.
3. Reimu stops the spectacle because the shrine is being damaged.
4. The protagonist refuses to name a winner.
5. Several characters correctly reject a vague “I love everyone” answer.
6. The group writes an actual schedule and privacy rules.
7. Comedy emerges from negotiations:
   - visiting hours;
   - no unannounced portals in bedrooms;
   - no stopping time to gain extra turns;
   - food budget;
   - publication consent;
   - spell-card-only conflict;
   - emergency medical authority.
8. Quiet ending: no grand pile-on. Each participant leaves one practical sign of continued presence.

## Non-Accord ensemble ending

If prerequisites are not met, the group remains a close community. The protagonist is not punished for choosing exclusivity, friendship, or uncertainty.

## Dialogue rule

Possessive boasts may appear as theatrical style:
- Remilia's command language;
- Tenshi's provocation;
- Yukari's boundary metaphors.

The narrative must show that actual consent remains negotiable.
