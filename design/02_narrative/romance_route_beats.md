# Deep Romance Route Beats

## Reimu Hakurei — “The Empty Cushion”

1. **A Guest Who Was Already Expected**  
   Reimu denies remembering the protagonist while having prepared tea for two. The player helps clear boundary residue from the veranda.

2. **Offerings Without Owners**  
   The donation box receives memories instead of coins. Reimu is less interested in profit than in restoring proper shrine function, undercutting the obvious joke.

3. **The Day Nothing Happens**  
   A no-slot event: sweep, mend paper screens, sit. The route advances only if the player tolerates silence.

4. **A Shrine Is Not a Home, Except When It Is**  
   Reimu resists the protagonist treating the shrine as a domestic refuge while ignoring its dangerous boundary duty.

5. **The Unasked Rescue**  
   Reimu protects the protagonist during a boundary collapse, then is annoyed when thanked too dramatically. She later accepts help repairing the damage.

6. **Perfectly Recorded Tea**  
   The Archive offers to preserve every quiet afternoon. Reimu rejects an afternoon that cannot surprise her or run out of tea.

7. **Promise**  
   Friendship: “Come by when you feel like it.”  
   Romance: she moves the protagonist's cushion inside before rain, saying only, “You're staying, aren't you?”

## Marisa Kirisame — “Return Before Borrowing”

1. **Crash Landing**  
   Marisa arrives with three borrowed books and one returned object. Broom Backseat tutorial.

2. **Field Notes in the Forest**  
   The player learns that her reckless-looking experiments contain careful records and repeated failures.

3. **The Shelf Marked Later**  
   Her house is crowded with materials saved for future use. The player may clean, catalog, or ask before touching.

4. **Talent Is a Bad Conversation**  
   A comparison with Reimu lands badly. Playful deflection fails; honest respect for effort is required.

5. **The Rescue That Looks Like a Race**  
   Marisa leads the protagonist through dangerous weather, constantly joking until the danger has passed.

6. **The Infinite Experiment**  
   The Archive offers perfect recall of every failed spell, removing the need to struggle. Marisa refuses because the next attempt would no longer be hers.

7. **Promise**  
   Friendship: a permanent seat on the broom, “provided you hold the bags.”  
   Romance: she returns a Keepsake instead of borrowing it, then asks whether she can borrow the protagonist's tomorrow.

## Sakuya Izayoi — “One Unfinished Minute”

1. **Late by Three Minutes**  
   Sakuya states the exact delay and silently adjusts the schedule. Perfect Tea Service tutorial.

2. **A Corridor With No Dust**  
   The protagonist finds a corridor reset so often that no one remembers walking through it.

3. **Kitchen After Midnight**  
   Sakuya is alone, working without an audience. The player may help only after asking.

4. **Competence Is Not Consent**  
   The protagonist relies on her ability without checking whether she wants the task. Strain event.

5. **The Favor She Cannot Optimize**  
   Sakuya asks the protagonist to choose a tea for Remilia because efficiency cannot answer preference.

6. **The Missing Minute**  
   The Archive offers one perfect stopped minute where no duty can intrude. She returns it to the world.

7. **Promise**  
   Friendship: a pocket watch ribbon and permission to visit the kitchen after hours.  
   Romance: she schedules nothing for one evening and says, “This time is not work.”

## Remilia Scarlet — “A Fate With Room for Error”

1. **The Audience**  
   Remilia stages the protagonist's arrival as though foretold. The player can participate without pretending to believe every claim.

2. **Red Mist Etiquette**  
   Escort guests through status-sensitive silhouettes.

3. **The Small Chair Behind the Throne**  
   The protagonist discovers where Remilia sits when she is not performing authority.

4. **Do Not Laugh at the Wrong Thing**  
   A joke punctures charisma in public. Repair requires restoring dignity without infantilizing her.

5. **The Fate She Does Not Announce**  
   Remilia quietly changes a mansion plan to protect the protagonist, then refuses credit.

6. **Predestination Exhibit**  
   The Archive offers proof that every meeting was fate. Remilia destroys the proof because proclaimed destiny is more interesting when it can fail.

7. **Promise**  
   Friendship: standing invitation to midnight tea.  
   Romance: “I have decided our fate,” followed by the softer, private addition, “You may still say no.”

## Patchouli Knowledge — “The Page Left Blank”

1. **Library Breathing Room**  
   Dust, books, and element reactions create a logistics puzzle.

2. **A Question Worth Asking**  
   Patchouli dismisses trivia but engages deeply with a precise, sincere question.

3. **Shared Silence, Different Books**  
   A quiet event rewards not interrupting. Companion comments are disabled.

4. **The Borrowing Argument**  
   Marisa's habits create conflict. The protagonist must avoid treating either woman as a caricature.

5. **A Spell Written for Two Readers**  
   Patchouli asks the protagonist to hold an unstable conceptual “margin” while she casts.

6. **The Book That Answers Everything**  
   The Archive creates a volume containing every future conversation. Patchouli leaves its last pages blank.

7. **Promise**  
   Friendship: a reserved reading desk.  
   Romance: she writes the protagonist's name on a borrowing card that has no due date, then immediately adds rules.

## Youmu Konpaku — “What the Sword Cannot Finish”

1. **Garden Shift**  
   Soul Garden tutorial; Youmu treats gardening with combat seriousness.

2. **Two Bodies, One Embarrassment**  
   Half-Phantom Balance puzzle creates visual comedy without sexualizing the phantom half.

3. **A Duty Delegated**  
   Youmu struggles to let the protagonist watch one small section of the garden.

4. **Cutting the Wrong Problem**  
   She attempts to solve an emotional dispute as a task. The player must resist becoming her commander.

5. **The Meal She Gets to Finish**  
   Yuyuko deliberately takes over a duty so Youmu can sit through an entire meal.

6. **A Farewell That Will Not Fall**  
   The Archive freezes petals and spirits. Youmu learns that guarding something includes allowing its ending.

7. **Promise**  
   Friendship: a practice sword and morning training invitation.  
   Romance: “There are things I cannot cut. I would like to stop calling that a failure.”

## Yuyuko Saigyouji — “The Last Bite”

1. **Bottomless Banquet**  
   Appetite-management comedy teaches the player to read her conversational cues.

2. **A Joke About Death**  
   The protagonist laughs, then notices the joke protected someone else from fear.

3. **The Empty Plate**  
   A quiet scene where Yuyuko remembers a meal only through the person who shared it.

4. **Do Not Treat Lightness as Shallowness**  
   The player pushes for seriousness at the wrong moment. Yuyuko refuses the imposed mood.

5. **A Host's Responsibility**  
   She protects Youmu and guests by redirecting a dangerous spirit procession through a festival.

6. **The Feast Without an Ending**  
   Infinite food loses taste. Yuyuko ends the banquet herself.

7. **Promise**  
   Friendship: “Come hungry, but bring a story.”  
   Romance: she saves the final sweet, an act everyone present recognizes as extraordinary.

## Aya Shameimaru — “The Photograph Not Printed”

1. **Exclusive Interview**  
   The player chooses which parts of an arrival story are on record.

2. **Wind-Frame Graze**  
   A photo-danmaku tutorial where good composition requires proximity to danger.

3. **The Hidden Folder**  
   Not a secret cache of romantic photos; rather, unpublished images where intervention mattered more than a headline.

4. **The Story She Ruins by Publishing**  
   Aya prints too soon. Repair requires correction, not simply deleting evidence.

5. **Camera Down**  
   The protagonist witnesses Aya exhausted after racing the news cycle.

6. **Tomorrow's Front Page**  
   The Archive provides a perfect future headline. Aya withholds it, accepting uncertainty.

7. **Promise**  
   Friendship: shared byline on a corrective article.  
   Romance: she asks permission before taking a portrait and keeps the first approved print for herself.

## Eirin Yagokoro — “A Treatment With an End Date”

1. **Clinic Triage**  
   Diagnose ordinary patients while Tewi introduces misleading symptoms.

2. **The Patient Who Says No**  
   Eirin respects a refusal, establishing that competence includes consent.

3. **An Immortal's Practical Care**  
   She treats a small human injury with the same attention as a cosmic problem.

4. **Do Not Volunteer as an Experiment**  
   The protagonist offers reckless self-sacrifice. Eirin is genuinely angry.

5. **The Doctor Sleeps**  
   Kaguya and Reisen conspire to give Eirin one uninterrupted rest period.

6. **Permanent Cure**  
   The Archive offers to remove mortality, uncertainty, and diagnostic error. Eirin rejects a cure that erases the patient.

7. **Promise**  
   Friendship: a health ledger written for an ordinary lifespan.  
   Romance: “I will not promise forever. I will promise the next appointment, and arrive.”

## Kaguya Houraisan — “One Finite Evening”

1. **Five Impossible Errands**  
   Each request can be solved literally, cleverly, or by refusing the premise.

2. **Elegance and Boredom**  
   Kaguya moves between formal performance and playful challenge without becoming a two-note NEET joke.

3. **A Game With No Continue**  
   She chooses a simple one-life game and takes the loss seriously.

4. **The Short-Lived Guest**  
   Mortality becomes explicit. The protagonist must not promise impossible equivalence.

5. **Mokou's Uninvited Honesty**  
   Their conflict reveals how immortality distorts closeness and rivalry.

6. **The Endless Night Offer**  
   The Archive can preserve one evening forever. Kaguya lets dawn arrive.

7. **Promise**  
   Friendship: a standing challenge with changing rules.  
   Romance: “I cannot make your life eternal. I can stop treating its moments as replaceable.”

## Sanae Kochiya — “A Miracle With Witnesses”

1. **Faith Festival Planner**  
   Balance spectacle, safety, and sincere worship.

2. **Outside World Shorthand**  
   Sanae assumes shared modern context; the protagonist notices how much she has adapted.

3. **A Shrine Between Homes**  
   A low-key event about homesickness without making her merely nostalgic.

4. **Publicity Versus Belief**  
   A campaign succeeds numerically and fails spiritually.

5. **The Ordinary Miracle**  
   Sanae helps without invoking divine power and is unsettled by how meaningful it feels.

6. **Guaranteed Faith**  
   The Archive offers measurable belief. She rejects worship that cannot doubt.

7. **Promise**  
   Friendship: exchange of Outside World recipes and shrine duties.  
   Romance: a photograph taken only after she asks whether this moment should be shared.

## Tenshi Hinanawi — “Ask Before the Earth Moves”

1. **The Entrance Tremor**  
   Tenshi causes a small disturbance to announce herself. The player challenges the damage, not her existence.

2. **Keystone Construction**  
   Physics puzzle where overbuilding is as bad as underbuilding.

3. **An Imperfect Meal on the Ground**  
   She enjoys ordinary food while criticizing it with transparent enthusiasm.

4. **Attention Is Not Permission**  
   Tenshi breaks something to force engagement. The route requires a firm boundary.

5. **The Repair She Finishes**  
   Without an audience, she rebuilds what she damaged and does not advertise it.

6. **A Heaven Without Friction**  
   The Archive offers permanent stimulation and perfectly calibrated challenge. She rejects a world unable to surprise or refuse her.

7. **Promise**  
   Friendship: a formal challenge contract.  
   Romance: “Then keep looking at me—but tell me when I have gone too far.”
