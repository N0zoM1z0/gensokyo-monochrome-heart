# Touhou Fan-Work Guideline Notes

Research snapshot: 2026-07-17. This summary is not legal advice and does not replace the current official guideline page.

The release-candidate review opened the official
[Touhou Project fan-work guideline](https://touhou-project.news/guideline/)
on 2026-07-17. The page is dated 2024-05-31 and states that individual fan
activity is generally permitted within common sense, requires clear fan-work
identification, prohibits misleading official presentation and infringement of
others' rights, and limits distribution to original-sales or permitted channels.
It also says the guideline may change without notice. This candidate therefore
does not select a storefront, price, crowdfunding plan, or public support
channel by implication; each needs an owner-approved review immediately before
distribution.

## Production interpretation

- Clearly identify the game as an unofficial Touhou Project fan work.
- Do not present Team Shanghai Alice or ZUN as a participant, sponsor, or publisher.
- Create or properly license all implementation assets.
- Do not extract official game data, sprites, music, SFX, fonts, scripts, endings, or code.
- Do not use another fan creator's art, arrangement, translation, or design without permission.
- Recheck distribution platform, pricing, crowdfunding, and monetization against current rules at every public milestone.
- Keep a permission/provenance archive for guest work.
- Ensure store/title presentation cannot be mistaken for an official release.
- Credit Touhou Project and Team Shanghai Alice in the form required/appropriate under the current rules.

## Conservative project rules

Even where fan-work rules may allow broad creativity, this project additionally requires:
- all-ages / PG-13 base release;
- no sexualized marketing around ambiguously aged characters;
- no paid romance, gacha, or loot boxes;
- no official asset used even as a hidden placeholder;
- no runtime AI-generated dialogue;
- explicit Dream Theatre labeling for high-fanon AU material;
- original or licensed music production with cue-level rights records.

## Release review checklist

1. Open the current official guidelines, not a cached summary.
2. Record review date and reviewer.
3. Compare game title/logo/store capsule for official-confusion risk.
4. Audit every asset hash against provenance records.
5. Audit music recordings and arrangements.
6. Audit guest translations and fonts.
7. Confirm permitted storefront/distributor and payment model.
8. Confirm demo, soundtrack, crowdfunding, and physical goods separately.
9. Confirm credits/unofficial notice in EN and JA.
10. Preserve the review record with the release manifest.
