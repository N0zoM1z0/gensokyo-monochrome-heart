# Music Reference Register

The full 89-cue mapping is in `07_audio/music_cue_sheet.md` and `.csv`.

## Why references are listed

Touhou music is a major part of fan recognition. The cue sheet helps a composer understand which official melodic identity best fits a location, character, or emotional beat. It does not include audio or authorize a recording.

## Title policy

- Japanese title is the primary lookup key.
- English translations vary; the cue sheet uses familiar community renderings.
- Source work is recorded for credit preparation.
- Before final credits, verify title spelling, punctuation, composer credit, and source against an official/authoritative record.

## Arrangement policy

A reference can become a shipped cue only after one of these paths is approved:
- in-house original arrangement and recording;
- commissioned arrangement with written game-use rights;
- directly licensed existing arrangement with compatible rights;
- replacement by original composition.

The arrangement must not be a sound-alike reconstruction of an official recording. Chip constraints do not excuse extraction or transcription from a ripped file.

## Research confidence

High confidence:
- character boss themes and stage themes from official music rooms;
- titles with stable Japanese names;
- source game association.

Review required:
- English title variants;
- themes first released on print-work CDs;
- medley permissions/credits;
- soundtrack release rights separate from in-game rights;
- platform automated-content-claim behavior.

## Original fallback motifs

The project has original motif families for:
- Monochrome Journal;
- memory stains;
- rain/shared shelter;
- winter domestic scenes;
- comic escalation;
- quiet afterbeat;
- Outside World train/phone;
- Archive boss connective tissue.

This makes the game buildable even if a particular arrangement cannot be licensed or completed.
