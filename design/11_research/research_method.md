# Research Method

## 1. Question hierarchy

For every character or place, research answers in this order:

1. What does an official profile explicitly say?
2. What does official dialogue repeatedly demonstrate?
3. What do official print works add or complicate?
4. What relationships are shown rather than merely inferred?
5. Which popular traits are fan interpretations?
6. What project-original behavior can connect the anchors without contradiction?

## 2. Source hierarchy

### Tier A — Primary official
- game omake profiles and manuals;
- in-game dialogue and endings available through owned/authorized sources;
- official spell-card and music-room text;
- official print works and CDs by/under Team Shanghai Alice.

### Tier B — Official-adjacent translation/navigation
- reputable translation projects and reference databases used to locate passages;
- checked against Japanese wording for high-impact claims.

### Tier C — Community secondary
- Touhou Wiki and other mature fan references for discovery, title variants, cross-links, and fanon identification;
- never sufficient alone for a disputed personality claim.

### Tier D — Fan culture
- memes, doujin conventions, common pairings, nicknames, and derivative archetypes;
- labeled fanon and given an intensity ceiling.

## 3. Evidence rule

A profile may say “Official dialogue repeatedly presents…” only when multiple official scenes support the description. A single line is noted as an example, not a universal trait.

Absence of canon is marked as sparse. It is not filled with the most popular fan depiction by default.

## 4. Translation rule

- official Japanese phrasing has priority;
- English names/titles may vary by translation database;
- the package uses familiar English forms for production readability, not as legal titles;
- Japanese dialogue samples are original cadence exercises, not quotations;
- no long official dialogue is reproduced.

## 5. Relationship rule

A relationship edge records observable structure: friend, rival, mistress/retainer, sibling, mentor, subordinate, faction opponent. Romantic interpretation is never marked canon unless explicitly supported. Project romance starts from character-compatible trust development.

## 6. Fanon dial

0 — no fanon use.  
1 — background wink.  
2 — one scene mechanic or joke, immediately rebalanced by character depth.  
3 — optional comedy event.  
4 — Dream Theatre/AU only.  
5 — intentionally fanon parody, never main continuity.

Each character profile has a maximum. Event data should also record actual intensity.

## 7. Review triggers

Reopen research when:
- a new official work materially features the character;
- a writer proposes a relationship not in the graph;
- a fan joke becomes a plot premise;
- a translation changes a named ability or relationship reading;
- a route finale depends on a disputed claim;
- public feedback identifies a likely canon error.
