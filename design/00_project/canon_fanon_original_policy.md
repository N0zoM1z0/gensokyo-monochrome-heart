# Canon, Fanon, and Project-Original Policy

Every character and event claim is tagged internally with one of three levels.

## C — Canon anchor
Directly supported by an official game profile, official dialogue, or official print work.

Use for:
- species, residence, role, named ability
- major relationships
- recurring speech habits visible in official dialogue
- responsibilities and incident motives

## F — Common fanon
A widespread community interpretation or joke that is not fully established by official works.

Use only when:
- the joke does not overwrite a canon anchor;
- the scene still works for a player who does not know the meme;
- the character's `fanon_dial` permits it;
- the script does not state it as objective fact.

## O — Project original
A new habit, event, relationship beat, gameplay interpretation, or line written for this game.

Use freely when:
- it follows the character contract;
- it does not claim to be official;
- it serves the current story rather than trivia display.

## Confidence labels

Character files use:
- **High:** repeated direct official support
- **Medium:** reasonable inference from multiple official appearances
- **Low:** sparse characterization; writer must keep claims modest

## Examples

- Reimu's role as Hakurei shrine maiden: **C / High**
- Reimu being permanently destitute and charging for every smile: **F / Medium**
- Reimu keeping a repaired cushion for the protagonist: **O**
- Kaguya as a modern shut-in gamer: **F**
- Kaguya using games to avoid thinking about eternity in this project: **O**, built from **C** themes of eternity and exile
- Koakuma as a named succubus with a detailed backstory: unsupported; do not use as canon

## Writer rule

When a scene depends on a fan joke, add one canon-compatible second layer. The joke opens the door; character truth closes the scene.
