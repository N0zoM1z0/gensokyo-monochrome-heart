# Design Principles and Decision Rules

## The five-question test for every feature

Before adding a mechanic, scene, or joke, answer:

1. **Whose desire drives it?**
2. **Why can it happen only at this location?**
3. **What does the player do, not merely watch?**
4. **What changes in a relationship or world state?**
5. **Can it be expressed clearly in one-bit art?**

If two or more answers are weak, cut or redesign it.

## Spot Event grammar

Every authored event follows a reusable grammar:

- **Hook:** a visible contradiction, rumor, request, or accident
- **Social read:** choose an approach: Direct, Playful, Patient, or Defiant
- **Investigation:** 2D side-view movement, examination, lightweight platforming
- **Mode turn:** minigame, danmaku, or duel
- **Consequence:** world-state, relationship-state, item, rumor, or route flag
- **Afterbeat:** a short low-noise exchange
- **Echo:** a later event references the player's behavior

## Tone choices instead of “correct dialogue”

The player normally chooses intent, not exact words:

- **Direct** — honest, efficient, potentially blunt
- **Playful** — joins the character's bit without surrendering judgment
- **Patient** — listens, waits, or helps quietly
- **Defiant** — challenges pride, manipulation, or avoidance

Characters react to different tones. No tone is universally optimal.

## Comedy rules

- Use repetition with variation, not repeated catchphrases.
- Let a character win jokes sometimes.
- Build escalation from a plausible motive.
- End a large gag with one precise human detail.
- Do not turn poverty, illness, disability, or trauma into the only joke.
- “Fanon dial” values in character files cap how strongly a meme may be used.

## Romance rules

- No route begins with instant devotion.
- Affection does not remove disagreement.
- Power imbalance must be acknowledged in writing and mechanics.
- A route climax is a choice to share responsibility, not possession.
- The ensemble ending is a negotiated community ending, not a conquest ending.
- The protagonist may decline romance while retaining friendship content.

## Difficulty rules

- Normal mode is intended for narrative players.
- Focus mode enlarges telegraphs and slows dense patterns without changing story rewards.
- No romance content is locked behind Lunatic difficulty.
- Failure should generate characterful recovery dialogue, not shame.
- Every minigame can be skipped after two failures, with a reduced mechanical reward but full narrative continuity.

## One-bit readability rules

- Silhouettes must remain distinguishable at 1× scale.
- A bullet may never share the same local texture frequency as the background.
- Interactive objects use a two-frame shimmer rather than gray color.
- Dither patterns have semantic meaning and are listed in the art bible.
- Photosensitivity options disable high-frequency flashing and checkerboard inversion.
