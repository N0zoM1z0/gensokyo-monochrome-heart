# Content Rating and Boundaries

## Target

All-ages / PG-13 romantic comedy.

## Allowed

- flirting, crushes, embarrassment, hand-holding, hugs, implied dates
- stylized fantasy combat and non-graphic impact
- alcohol shown as part of Gensokyo social life, without pressuring the player to drink
- melancholy themes: mortality, memory, loneliness, duty
- fandom in-jokes that remain intelligible without prior knowledge

## Not allowed in the base game

- explicit sexual content
- sexualized treatment of age-ambiguous characters
- non-consensual romance framed as a reward
- fetishized restraint, intoxication, medical treatment, or power imbalance
- graphic injury or body horror
- routes that reward coercion
- harassment as the only expression of affection

## Adaptation policy for the source conversation

The conversation archive contains escalating harem comedy, fanon exaggeration, and occasional suggestive jokes. Adaptation follows four filters:

1. Preserve the **comic premise** where useful.
2. Replace explicit or coercive framing with mutual, character-led play.
3. Preserve the **quiet emotional afterbeat**.
4. Mark the result as project-original rather than canonical.

Examples:
- “fatal chocolate” becomes a taste-testing and status-effect minigame.
- “mixed bathing” becomes a public hot-spring logistics incident with privacy screens and slapstick.
- “everyone fights over the protagonist” becomes a cooperative scheduling or rumor-management problem.
- “ownership” dialogue is treated as theatrical boasting unless a character file explicitly explains the boundary.

## Player comfort settings

- romance on/off per character
- ensemble route on/off
- alcohol references reduced
- flirt intensity: low / standard
- screen shake, flash, dither motion, and hit-stop sliders
- content notes before mortality-focused chapters
