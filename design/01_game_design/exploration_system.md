# Side-View Exploration System

## Camera and movement

- side-view 2D
- 320 × 180 internal resolution
- walking, short hop, drop-through, inspect, speak, focus
- no precision platforming required for story progress
- 8-direction interaction probe while standing
- optional auto-walk during dialogue

## Character scale

Field avatar target:
- 24 × 40 pixels for protagonist
- 24–32 × 40–48 pixels for major characters
- 16 × 24 mini avatars on the map
- silhouettes readable without facial detail

## Focus mode

Holding Focus:
- slows walking;
- reveals interaction outlines;
- displays sound, wind, scent, or spiritual traces as character-specific dither marks;
- allows one-line internal observations;
- does not freeze time.

## Companion behavior

One companion may follow during route events.

Companions:
- have 3–5 context actions;
- comment on points of interest;
- may refuse an action consistent with character;
- never solve the entire scene automatically;
- can alter traversal.

Examples:
- Marisa boosts across a gap but lands noisily.
- Sakuya creates a temporary stopped object platform.
- Youmu cuts vines but may refuse to cut a memorial marker.
- Aya rides an updraft and reveals a photo angle.
- Reisen reveals wavelength echoes.
- Yukari folds two screen edges together in late game.

## Environmental language

Interactive cues:
- two-frame sparkle: ordinary inspect
- alternating outline: required object
- moving dither: supernatural trace
- inverted one-pixel halo: companion interaction
- broken frame corner: boundary instability

## Puzzle principles

- 1–3 concepts per event
- no pixel hunting
- reset within two seconds
- companion solution hints are in voice
- every puzzle reveals character or place information
- avoid generic key-and-lock puzzles unless the key has social meaning

## Collectibles

No map is covered in meaningless items.

Collect:
- **Margin Notes:** optional observations that expand the Journal
- **Lost Headlines:** Aya/Hatate rumor fragments
- **Tea Ingredients:** one-use daily modifiers
- **Broken Charms:** unlock challenge patterns
- **Quiet Objects:** route-specific keepsakes

## Exploration failure

There are no lethal pits. Falling returns the player with a character reaction and a tiny time cost. Repeated failure turns on an unobtrusive route line.
