# World Map and Spot Design

## Map form

The world map is a hand-drawn, one-bit folding map. Regions appear as large ink islands connected by paths, wind lines, river marks, and border tears. It is not geographically authoritative; it is the protagonist's evolving mental map.

At 320 × 180:
- 5–7 visible region icons per map page;
- cursor travel animates as a one-pixel paper token;
- undiscovered regions appear as dithered voids;
- rumors pulse by alternating two dither masks, not by color.

## Region unlock philosophy

Regions unlock through social knowledge:
- a character gives directions;
- a newspaper publishes a route;
- a spell-card duel proves safe passage;
- a Keepsake reveals a hidden path;
- a rumor is corrected.

## Spot taxonomy

- **Home:** daily conversations, crafting, letters
- **Threshold:** gates, paths, stairs, bridges; good for first encounters
- **Workplace:** duties and competence become visible
- **Private corner:** route afterbeats
- **Rumor site:** unstable, repeats with variation
- **Arena:** fighter-friendly geometry
- **Sky lane:** danmaku transition
- **Festival site:** ensemble event container

## Spot density

Per headline region:
- 1 home hub
- 3 permanent public spots
- 2 route-sensitive private spots
- 1 action arena
- 1 seasonal overlay
- 8–15 authored events at launch

## Fast travel

Unlocked after first physical journey. Fast travel still consumes the appropriate time slot and may trigger:
- road encounter;
- newspaper interruption;
- invitation collision;
- weather change.

The player can disable random road encounters.
