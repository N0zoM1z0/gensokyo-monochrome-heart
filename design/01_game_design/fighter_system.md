# Compact Fighting System

## Intent

A readable two-dimensional duel inspired by Touhou fighting spin-offs, re-scoped for narrative events and one-bit art.

## Match format

- 1 vs 1
- best of three rounds for challenge mode
- one round for story events
- 60–90 seconds per round
- health plus Temperament Gauge
- no tag system at launch

## Controls

- movement: left/right, crouch, jump
- Light
- Heavy
- Spell
- Guard / Dash
- optional simplified input mode

No quarter-circle inputs are required. Direction + button combinations provide depth without excluding narrative players.

## Universal actions

- ground dash
- air dash, character-dependent count
- high/low guard
- throw / close interaction
- Focus Cancel: spend Temperament to recover early
- Spell Declaration: temporary stance that unlocks two stronger techniques

## Temperament Gauge

Gain through:
- movement that matches character identity;
- successful guard;
- taking calculated risk;
- stage interactions.

Examples:
- Reimu gains from near-miss movement and neutral resets.
- Marisa gains from forward momentum.
- Sakuya gains from precise timing.
- Youmu gains from stance transitions.
- Tenshi gains from absorbing terrain shock.
- Aya gains from speed variety.

## Incident Conditions

Instead of random weather, story duels use a declared temporary rule:

- **Red Mist:** far silhouettes become hidden.
- **Spring Snow:** jumps float longer.
- **Mountain Gale:** air movement is stronger.
- **False Moon:** left/right input briefly mirrors after marked attacks.
- **Hungry Ghosts:** dropped food restores whoever reaches it.
- **Boundary Leak:** screen edges connect for projectiles only.
- **Temperament Storm:** both gauges fill faster but guard damage rises.

## Story integration

A duel is appropriate when:
- two characters need equal agency;
- the argument is too embodied for dialogue;
- spell-card etiquette makes conflict safe;
- losing can still be narratively meaningful.

Do not use a duel simply because a scene needs excitement.

## Sample move kit format

Each fighter has:
- 5 normals
- 2 command normals
- 4 specials
- 2 declared spells
- 1 passive
- 1 stage interaction preference
- 1 narrative surrender animation

## Accessibility

- simplified inputs
- adjustable hit-stop and screen shake
- training display
- optional one-button auto-combo that deals reduced damage
- story “Resolve” option after two losses
